üåì Introduction to Lunar Phases in Hindu Calendar

The Lunar-Based Calendar System

The Hindu calendar is lunar-based, with each month divided into two distinct parts based on the moon's visibility and phases.

üåî What is Shukla Paksha?

Etymology & Meaning

"Shukla" means bright, white, or pure - referring to the increasing illumination of the moon each night.

üåô Astronomical Details

  • Begins with Amavasya (New Moon)
  • Ends with Purnima (Full Moon)
  • Moon visibility: 0% ‚Üí 100%
  • Duration: Approximately 15 days
  • Moon rises later each day

üåò What is Krishna Paksha?

Etymology & Meaning

"Krishna" means dark or black - referring to the diminishing light of the moon as it wanes towards complete darkness.

üåô Astronomical Details

  • Ends with Amavasya (New Moon)
  • Moon visibility: 100% ‚Üí 0%
  • Duration: Approximately 15 days
  • Moon rises earlier each day

üìÜ Significance in Hindu Culture

Festival Calendar Foundation

Most Hindu festivals are precisely timed according to specific tithis within these pakshas, demonstrating the deep astronomical knowledge of ancient scholars.

üåî Shukla Paksha Festivals

  • Diwali - Kartik Amavasya (after)
  • Holi - Phalguna Purnima
  • Ram Navami - Chaitra Navami
  • Guru Purnima - Ashadha Purnima
  • Navratri - Ashwin/Chaitra

üåò Krishna Paksha Festivals

  • Krishna Janmashtami - Bhadrapada Ashtami
  • Maha Shivratri - Phalguna Chaturdashi
  • Pitru Paksha - Bhadrapada Krishna Paksha
  • Karva Chauth - Kartik Chaturthi
  • Ganga Dussehra - Jyeshtha Dashami

Muhurat Calculation: Tithis (Lunar Dates) during Pakshas are used to determine auspicious days (Muhurats) for ceremonies, weddings, and important life events.

Special Observances

Ekadashi Fasting

Both pakshas have Ekadashi (11th tithi) - considered highly auspicious for spiritual practices and fasting. Many people follow vrats (fasts) on these days.

Ashtami Observances

The 8th tithi in both pakshas is significant - Durga Ashtami in Shukla Paksha and Krishna Janmashtami in Krishna Paksha.
